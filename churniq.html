01 — The problem

Revenue leaves quietly. Most teams only find out at cancel.

SaaS businesses shed 5–7% of accounts a month. By the time a customer hits "cancel," the window to intervene is already closed — and the team never knew which lever would have changed their mind.

No.1
No visibility into who will leave
Churn shows up in a dashboard after it happens. There's no forward signal, no ranking, no time to act.
No.2
No explanation for why
Even with a risk score, every at-risk account looks identical. Teams can't tell a pricing problem from an onboarding one.
No.3
No personalized action
"We miss you" emails don't work. Worse, discounts get burned on customers who were never going to leave.

03 — Core capabilities

Three systems, one outcome.

Predict who leaves before they do

An XGBoost classifier on 21 engineered features returns a calibrated churn probability for every active account. SMOTE resolves the 26.5% minority-class imbalance that makes naive models predict "stays" for everyone.

  • 21 behavioral + billing features from raw data
  • Risk bands: low / medium / high, configurable thresholds
  • Beats logistic regression & random forest on AUC and calibration

Know exactly why each account is at risk

SHAP TreeExplainer produces per-customer attributions — not just global importance. Every score carries a ranked list of what pushed it up (warm) or held it down (cool).

  • Local explanations per customer, exposed in UI and API
  • Warm bars raise risk, cool bars protect against it
  • Drives the LLM so emails reference real, specific reasons

Personalized retention, at scale

A T-learner first filters to the accounts a touch will actually move. Then Groq (Llama 3.1) drafts a ~120-word email grounded in that customer's SHAP drivers — handed back through FastAPI for any CRM.

  • Causal uplift filters to true intervention candidates
  • Intervention chosen by segment + risk band
  • POST /agent → plugs into HubSpot, Salesforce, anything

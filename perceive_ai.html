01 · Overview

From signal noise to emotional clarity

Built for a real gap: support tools that are blind to how a person actually feels. Integration is the moat — not a novel architecture.

The problem

Single-modal systems miss the room

A poker face fools vision. Calm text hides visible distress. Neither channel alone is enough.

The solution

Late-fusion with explainability

Two encoders feed a weighted attention layer. One embedding drives emotion, risk and a recommendation.

The approach

Pretrained backbones, no retraining

ViT on FER2013 and RoBERTa on GoEmotions, fused by a lightweight rule-based attention head.

The outputs

Emotion, valence, arousal, risk

7-class emotion plus continuous valence/arousal and a low/medium/high risk flag with a next action.

04 · Under the hood

Real models, honestly labelled

Vision encoder
ViT · Facial Expression

A Vision Transformer fine-tuned on FER2013, classifying 7 expressions and emitting a CLS embedding for fusion.

motheecreator/vit-Facial-Expression-Recognition
Text encoder
RoBERTa · GoEmotions

RoBERTa over GoEmotions' 28 labels, mapped down to the same 7 core emotions, plus a positive-probability sentiment score.

SamLowe/roberta-base-go_emotions
Fusion
Weighted attention · 55 / 45

Per-class probabilities combine at visual 0.55 / text 0.45. Valence blends emotion priors (70%) with text sentiment (30%); arousal and risk read from fixed per-emotion maps.

Honest scope
Integration over invention

FER2013 is a hard 7-class benchmark (human accuracy ≈ 65%). Numbers here come from the pretrained backbones, not a bespoke eval — the contribution is a clean, served, explainable fusion API in PyTorch + FastAPI.
